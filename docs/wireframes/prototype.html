Clinical Context

Define the clinical problem before applying machine learning. Understanding the medical context ensures meaningful results.

Selected Domain

Cardiology

Heart Failure Mortality Prediction
Clinical Question
Can we predict which heart failure patients are at higher risk of mortality based on clinical measurements such as ejection fraction, serum creatinine levels, and other diagnostic indicators?

Why This Matters

Heart failure affects over 64 million people worldwide. Early identification of high-risk patients enables timely interventions, personalised treatment plans, and improved resource allocation in clinical settings. Machine learning models can surface non-obvious risk patterns that traditional scoring systems may miss.

Medical Glossary
Quick reference for clinical and ML terms used in this tool
Ejection Fraction Clinical
Percentage of blood pumped out of the heart with each beat. Normal: 55-70%.
Serum Creatinine Clinical
Blood test measuring kidney function. High levels indicate kidney damage.
Sensitivity (Recall) ML
Proportion of actual positive cases correctly identified by the model.
Specificity ML
Proportion of actual negative cases correctly identified by the model.
SHAP Values ML
SHapley Additive exPlanations -- a method to explain individual model predictions.
SMOTE ML
Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique -- creates synthetic samples to balance classes.
AUC-ROC ML
Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic curve. Measures discrimination ability.
F1 Score ML
Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Balances both metrics.
Confusion Matrix ML
Table showing true/false positives and negatives of model predictions.
Overfitting ML
When a model memorizes training data and performs poorly on new data.
